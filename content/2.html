Why the Blazers won the 2023 NBA Trade Deadline



As the 2023 NBA Trade Deadline came to an end, it was the Portland Trailblazers that emerged as the big winner. With a series of savvy trades, the Blazers were able to acquire some key pieces that will help them compete in the Western Conference for years to come.

The first trade the Blazers made was to acquire forward Trevor Ariza from the Houston Rockets. Ariza is a proven veteran who has the ability to space the floor and shoot the three-pointer. He should provide an immediate boost to the Blazers’ offense and give them another reliable scoring option.

The Blazers then followed up the Ariza trade by sending center Jusuf Nurkic to the Cleveland Cavaliers. In return, the Blazers received center Andre Drummond, who is one of the best rebounders in the league. Drummond is a great fit in the Blazers’ system and should be able to provide them with the size and physicality they need in the paint.

The final trade the Blazers made was to acquire guard Derrick Rose from the New York Knicks. Rose is a former MVP who is still capable of playing at a high level. His presence should give the Blazers another scoring option and a reliable playmaker to rely on.

All in all, the Blazers had a great trade deadline and are now in a great position to compete in the Western Conference. With the addition of Ariza, Drummond, and Rose, the Blazers have a strong core group of players that should help them make a deep playoff run. It will be exciting to see how the Blazers use their new pieces and if they can live up to the expectations they have set.
